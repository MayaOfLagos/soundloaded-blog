Before you marry a person, make them use a computer with slow internet to see exactly who they are. If you remember dial up internet, you already passed a life test. That loud connection sound, waiting five whole minutes for one page to load, and praying nobody picked up the house phone. The second someone called, boom... you were kicked offline. Windows 98 did not care about your feelings or your patience. Now imagine going back to that after today's fast WiFi and instant streaming. No buffering, no spinning wheel, just pure frustration building slowly. It is funny because it is true. Technology can test anyone's patience. Not men, not women... just humans trying to stay calm while the screen refuses to cooperate. Maybe real compatibility is not about perfection but about how calmly you handle small annoyances together. Could you survive dial up days again without losing your cool?
